## Key Takeaways

- EA has transformed from static documentation into dynamic, near real-time decision support for intelligent AI adoption
- When hundreds of AI agents interact, they exhibit emergent behaviour diverging from expected norms — requiring "cluster governance" not just individual system oversight
- The University of Melbourne (Ern et al., 2025) proposes concrete TOGAF modifications: iterative feedback loops in ADM, AI lifecycle artifacts, drift detection, explainability modules
- Enterprise Architects should be change enablers, not gatekeepers — facilitating AI adoption rather than restricting it through bureaucratic oversight
- EA systems must function as continuously updated digital twins of enterprise operations, not static quarterly frameworks

## Summary

This article argues that enterprise architecture must fundamentally shift from periodic, documentation-heavy governance to near real-time digital governance to remain relevant in the age of AI. The author identifies five critical evolution areas: digital twin responsiveness, cluster governance for multi-agent systems, operational-strategic integration, cross-platform integration, and interdisciplinary workforce development.

A particularly noteworthy contribution is the concept of "cluster governance" — the idea that managing interactions among multiple GenAI agents collectively requires ethical audits and capability matrices that go beyond traditional per-system governance. When hundreds of AI agents interact, they can exhibit emergent behaviour that diverges from expected norms, necessitating new governance approaches.

The article cites research from the University of Melbourne (Ern, Yang, Wu, Ambakkat, Dilnutt & Meratian, 2025) proposing specific modifications to TOGAF: embedding iterative feedback loops within the ADM cycle, expanding artifacts to include AI lifecycle components (GenAI catalogs, drift detection, explainability modules), integrating ethical governance and regulatory compliance, and developing interdisciplinary roles combining AI literacy with strategic architecture expertise.
