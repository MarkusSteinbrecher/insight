## Structure

### Slide 1: Title
- Title of presentation
- Subtitle / tagline
- Author name and date

**Speaker notes**: Introduce yourself and the topic. Set expectations for what the audience will learn.

### Slide 2: The Problem / Opportunity
- One compelling statement about why this matters
- Supporting data point or visual

**Speaker notes**: Hook the audience. Why should they care about this right now?

### Slide 3: Context / Current State
- 3-4 bullet points establishing the landscape
- Key trend or market data

**Speaker notes**: Level-set the audience. What do they need to know to follow your argument?

### Slides 4-5: What Most People Think
- Prevailing assumptions or conventional wisdom
- Why these assumptions are incomplete or wrong

**Speaker notes**: Create tension. Show that the common understanding is missing something important.

### Slides 6-9: Core Insight (3-4 slides)
- One key point per slide
- Evidence or example supporting each point
- Visuals where possible (diagrams, charts, comparisons)

**Speaker notes**: This is the heart of the talk. Each slide should build on the previous one, constructing your argument step by step.

### Slide 10-11: Real-World Evidence
- Case study, example, or demonstration
- Concrete results or outcomes

**Speaker notes**: Ground the abstract insight in something tangible. Stories and examples are what people remember.

### Slide 12: Implications
- What this means for the audience
- 3-4 actionable takeaways

**Speaker notes**: The "so what." Connect back to their daily work or strategic decisions.

### Slide 13: Looking Ahead
- Where this is heading
- What to watch for

**Speaker notes**: Leave them with a forward-looking perspective. Make them feel ahead of the curve.

### Slide 14: Summary / Key Takeaways
- 3 bullet points capturing the core message
- One memorable closing statement

**Speaker notes**: Reinforce the main message. This is what you want them to remember tomorrow.

### Slide 15: Q&A / Discussion
- Contact information
- Link to full write-up or resources

**Speaker notes**: Transition to discussion. Have 2-3 provocative questions ready in case the audience is quiet.
